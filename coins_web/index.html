How to deal with your jars of idle HKD Coins before leaving Hong Kong?

1) HK Coin Collection Programme

A program offered by the HK government. The two Coin Carts collect coins from the public in the 18 districts of Hong Kong on a rotational basis and free of charge.

Users can choose to exchange coins for banknotes or adding value to their stored value facilities, such as Octopus Cards or e-wallets (including AlipayHK, Octopus O! ePay, Tap&Go, TNG Wallet and WeChat Pay). There is also a Community Chest donation box inside each vehicle.

Locations

2) Kiosk 1: HEYCOINS

HEYCOINS Kiosk collects coins from the general public and converts them into the digital currency that can be used for various purposes, including e-wallets recharge, gift coupons and charitable donations, etc.

Locations

3) Kiosk 2: Coin Dragon

Coin Dragon is another one-of-a-kind fully automated coin exchange kiosk in Hong Kong, which is similar to Heycoins.

Location

4) Luck

Bring your coins to convenient stores / restaurants / supermarkets or any other shops that use a lot of coins, and ask them if they want to exchange some equivalent HKD banknotes with you. The chance of getting a "Yes" is 50-50. Not a must to succeed because some shops may be busy and the staff or owners may not want to spend time counting coins. But in case the shop needs coins and they have time to do so, it would be a win-win situation (because normally they need to pay their coin suppliers (eg banks) to exchange for enough coins for operations). Good luck!

5) Banks (service fee)

Banks will charge you. The service fee can be counted up to 5% of the total coin values, depends on the bank you are using, and the value and number of coins you are going to exchange.

6) Give them to others

If you don't want to do any coin exchanges, you can give them to people who needs it or as a gift. They can be your Hong Kong friends that hosted you, needies, shops, colleagues, etc.
